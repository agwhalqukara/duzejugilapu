Professional Career

Vladimir was not always a media personality. He began his career by serving in the United States Army for three years, from 1988 to 1991. He served as the CDT Platoon Leader in the army. He retired from the military after three years of service. He then began working in the sales division for Shearson Lehman Brothers and Alliance Capital Management.

Vladimir then joined Alliance Bernstein in the year 1995. He joined the company as the assistant VP for the company. He held the position for over three years before earning a promotion to the work of Vice President as well as the Head of Sales Department.

He was able to place himself on the top three among the very best European Country Managers. He earned a second promotion and went on to hold the position of Managing Director. He had the position for sixteen years at the company.

Following this, Vladimir joined CNN in the year 2009 as a freelancer in the production department. He helped in researching and editing video segments and was also responsible for creating graphics. He then began his on-screen career in January of 2010.

He covered the Haiti earthquake alongside Anderson Cooper.  As of now, he is working as a correspondent for the CBS channel in Greater New York City. He began his journey with the team in 2014 and had been working with the team since then.
