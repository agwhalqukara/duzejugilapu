Dick Cheney Bio

Dick Cheney was born in Lincoln, Nebraska, the son of Marjorie Lorraine and Richard Herbert Cheney. Dick Cheney attended Yale University, but by his own account had problems adjusting to the college, and dropped out. In 1964, Dick Cheney married Lynne Vincent, his high school sweetheart, whom he had met at age 14.

Representing Wyoming in the U.S. House of Representatives

In 1978, Dick Cheney was elected to represent Wyoming in the U.S. House of Representatives and succeeded retiring Democratic Congressman Teno Roncalio, having defeated his Democratic opponent, Bill Bagley. Dick Cheney was re-elected five times, serving until 1989.

U.S. Senator John Heinz Award

President George H. W. Bush nominated Dick Cheney for the office of Secretary of Defense immediately after the U.S. Senate failed to confirm John Tower for that position. Later that year, Dick Cheney received the U.S. Senator John Heinz Award for Greatest Public Service by an Elected or Appointed Official, an award given out annually by Jefferson Awards.

Between 1987 and 1989, during his last term in Congress, Dick Cheney served on the board of the Council on Foreign Relations foreign policy organization. From October 1, 1995 to July 25, 2000, Dick Cheney served as chairman of the board and chief executive officer of Halliburton, a Fortune 500 company. Dick Cheney was also a member of the board of advisors of the Jewish Institute for National Security Affairs (JINSA) before becoming vice president.
